### Takeaways From Expo Presentation
I was number 14 to present in the expo and didn't go right away, so at first when visitors were taking a look at my project I took my time going through each slide and thoroughly explaining each visual aspect that I wanted to use in order for people to grasp the overall functionality while still showing enough of the code. 

When the actual judges came around, they set a timer for 1 minute and 40 seconds, but I didn't really have an elevaror pitch in mind so I basically ended up speeding through about 3/4 of my presentation, having to skip ahead throughout my video demo as well to meet the limited time-constrainsts. But I know that the judges still were able to clearly see and understand the functionality of my product right away when they saw the login feature and I skipped ahead throughout the create, delete, update and logout functionalities that I had time to showcase, since they gave me some verbal praise for my app while I was presenting the demo.

Overall, I feel like I could have simplified my explanantion and added a bit more diagrams, particularly in the part where I talk about the controllers being used to manipulate the `Note` and `User` object. I use a diagram in the slide after that explanantion to tie into how it can be modeled by that diagram, but perhaps the diagram could be shown along the way and not just after to make the explanation more effective and make it easier to visualize. There were a lot of visitors at the expo who were freshmen that never took SEP, so it is essential in the future to learn how to un-absract the code, and make the functionality behind some of the code in the app be as clearly understandable as possible.

### Takeaways From In Class Presentation
When I gave the presentation to my APCSA class I already had some preperation prior and was able to keep the whole thing within a reasonable timeframe. I gave the whole presentation within 9 minutes, and Mr. Mueller gave me positive feedback on my live narration of my video demo, visuals, and use of diagrams to illustrate my point throughout my presentation. I also got good feedback on my takeaways, as I blended takeaways about technical details with broader takeaways that can be applied to other aspects of life besides working on projects that require coding.

Mr Mueller gave me feedback on my hook; perhaps going more into depth of why someone might want to make their own notes app or app in general. I could talk about how it allows you to sign in from any browser and have your data already saved, and how it is a free and open source appication with privacy and security advantages over traditional note taking apps. Lastly, Mr. Mueller gave me feedback on my explanation of the code, which he was said was good, and utilized good vocabulary and terminology which clearly showed my understanding of the code, but could once again be less abstracted in order for the majority of people to be able to understand it.
